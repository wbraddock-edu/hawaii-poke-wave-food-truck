Poke Bowl Nutrition: Is Poke Actually Healthy?

Home › Blog
By Lena NakamuraApril 25, 20254 min read

Poke has a reputation as a healthy, clean meal. But is that reputation deserved? We asked a registered dietitian to analyze our most popular bowls and the answer is nuanced but mostly good news.

The Core Nutritional Profile

Fresh ahi tuna is one of the most nutritionally dense foods you can eat. A 4-ounce serving of fresh ahi contains approximately 130 calories, 28g of lean protein, and very low fat — mostly heart-healthy omega-3 fatty acids. Salmon is similarly impressive: slightly higher in calories (around 180 per 4oz) with excellent omega-3 content that supports heart and brain health.

Our Classic Ahi Wave Bowl: A Breakdown

A regular-size Classic Ahi Wave bowl contains approximately 520–580 calories, depending on the base. Macros break down roughly as: 32g protein, 72g carbohydrates (mostly from rice), 14g fat (mostly from avocado and sesame seeds). It provides significant amounts of vitamin D, B12, selenium, and magnesium.

Is that healthy? Absolutely. That's a balanced, nutrient-dense meal with excellent protein, complex carbs, healthy fats, and virtually no processed ingredients.

Where Calories Hide

The calorie count can climb quickly with certain additions: spicy mayo is calorie-dense (adds ~80–120 calories), crispy wonton strips add carbs and fat, and large bowl sizes can push totals toward 800+ calories. If you're calorie-conscious, ask for sauce on the side, choose brown rice or greens, and limit calorie-dense toppings.

The Healthiest Bowl We Make

Our Vegan Shore Bowl on a mixed greens base is the most calorie-friendly option at around 380–420 calories, and it's packed with fiber, antioxidants, and plant protein. For a more filling option with excellent nutrition, the Classic Ahi Wave on brown rice is hard to beat.

The Bottom Line

Poke made with fresh fish, simple seasonings, and whole-food toppings is genuinely healthy. It's one of the few fast-casual options where "healthy" and "delicious" fully align. Just be mindful of rich sauces and portion size.
