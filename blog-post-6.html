Kakaako's Food Truck Scene: Honolulu's Hottest Block

Home › Blog
By Kai NakamuraApril 10, 20255 min read

When we set up Poke Wave at Kakaako Waterfront three years ago, it was a quieter stretch of Ala Moana Blvd. Today, Kakaako is arguably the most exciting food destination in all of Honolulu, and its food truck culture is a huge part of why.

How Kakaako Became a Food Hub

The transformation of Kakaako from a warehouse district into Honolulu's creative hub started with Ward Village's residential developments and the influx of tech workers, artists, and young professionals who followed. With new residents came demand for diverse, affordable, high-quality food — and food trucks were perfectly positioned to serve that demand without the rent overhead of a brick-and-mortar restaurant.

The Kakaako Waterfront, which runs along Ala Moana Blvd near the entrance to Kakaako Park, became an unofficial food truck district. The city formalized it with dedicated truck parking and improved pedestrian infrastructure.

Best Trucks Beyond Poke Wave

We're friends with most of the other regulars and can honestly recommend them. For Hawaiian plate lunches, look for Uncle's Kitchen when they're parked up. For smash burgers, Honolulu Smash puts out an excellent double with loco moco vibes. The Filipino adobo truck whose name changes quarterly is always exceptional — if you see them, stop.

When to Visit

Lunch rush (11:30am–1:30pm) is when most trucks do peak volume. If you want no wait, come after 2pm when lines clear out. The evening vibe (5–7pm) on Fridays is particularly good — live music occasionally appears, families show up, and the sunset over the harbor is genuinely beautiful.

The KCC Connection

On Sunday mornings, many Kakaako trucks migrate to the KCC Farmers Market on Diamond Head Road, which runs from 7:30am to 1pm. This is the best farmers market in Honolulu bar none — incredible local produce, fresh fish, artisan goods, and yes, a very good poke bowl from us. Come early; popular items sell out.

Kakaako is still evolving. New trucks appear, some move on. But the energy and quality of the food scene here is the best in the city — and we're proud to be part of it.
